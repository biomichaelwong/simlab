! DSE Key Concepts

1. Why is velocity lowest in capillaries?

Although each capillary is narrow, the capillary bed is highly branched, making its total cross-sectional area the largest in the vascular system.
(Blood velocity is inversely proportional to total cross-sectional area)

2. Significance of slow velocity?

The slow blood flow provides a longer time for material exchange (e.g., oxygen diffusion) between the blood and body cells.

3. Why does blood pressure drop sharply?

Capillaries have very narrow lumens, which offer great resistance (friction) to blood flow, causing a significant drop in blood pressure.

4. Periodic changes in the arteriole

The wave-like blood pressure on the left is due to the blood being directly subjected to the pumping action of the heart. Pressure fluctuates with each heartbeat.
